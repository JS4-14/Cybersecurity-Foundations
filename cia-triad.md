# CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is one of the most fundamental concepts in cybersecurity. It consists of three principles that organisations aim to protect:

* Confidentiality
* Integrity
* Availability

Most security controls and cybersecurity practices exist to protect one or more of these principles.

## Confidentiality

Confidentiality ensures that sensitive information is only accessible to authorised individuals.

### Example

A hospital stores patient records containing medical histories, addresses and contact details.

If an unauthorised person gains access to these records:

* Patient privacy is breached
* Trust in the organisation is damaged
* Penalties may occur
* The information could be misused for fraud or identity theft

### Security Controls

Organisations commonly protect confidentiality through:

* Encryption
* Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
* Access controls and permissions
* Least privilege access

---

## Integrity

Integrity ensures that information remains accurate, complete and trustworthy.

Data should not be modified, deleted or altered by unauthorised individuals.

### Example

A customer's bank balance must remain accurate at all times.

If an attacker modifies account balances:

* Customers may lose money
* Financial records become unreliable
* The organisation may face legal and reputational consequences

Even accidental modifications can create significant problems if the integrity of the data is compromised.

### Security Controls

Organisations commonly protect integrity through:

* Hashing
* Validation checks
* Audit logs
* Change management processes
* Access controls

---

## Availability

Availability ensures that systems, services and data are accessible when required.

Users should be able to access services without interruption.

### Example

Online banking services must remain available so customers can make payments, transfer money and access their accounts.

If the service becomes unavailable:

* Customers cannot access their funds
* Business operations may be disrupted
* Financial losses may occur
* Trust in the service can decrease

Availability is especially important for important services such as healthcare and banking.

### Security Controls

Organisations protect availability through:

* Regular backups
* Redundant systems
* Disaster recovery plans
* Load balancing
* DDoS protection

---

## Summary

The CIA Triad provides a simple framework for understanding the goals of cybersecurity.

* Confidentiality protects information from unauthorised access.
* Integrity protects information from unauthorised modification.
* Availability ensures information and services remain accessible when needed.

When evaluating a security control, it is often useful to ask:

"Which part of the CIA Triad is this protecting?"
